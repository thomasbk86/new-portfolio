CASE STUDY

Capsule

While several sites exist for collecting and storing information and collaborating with peers, there is an opportunity to provide a more simple, user friendly experience. Capsule is a web application which allows people to collect information and store it in a single place. A person can create simple documents, store images, links to websites, and collaborate with others in simple and effective ways.

see the prototype

CHALLENGE create a responsive website to help users save and share images, links and text.

THE TASK design the brand and build the html, css, javaScript and jQuery of a website.

MY FOCUS brand creation and website design.

The Challenge

Designing for a responsive experience so users can access and view content in Capsule from any device. Simplicity in grouping and creating content, and being able to collaborate with ease was the focus when designing Capsule.

The Solution

There was an opportunity to focus on simplicity and the user experience to differentiate from competitors. After a careful examination of user research, the competitive analysis and various usability tests, I created Capsule - a place to collaborate with others and share ideas, links and images.

Process

Capsule started with gathering user research to build out personas and focus on the experience of using the site. After the personas were created, I moved to user flows, which set up the structure of the site. From there, I created the wireframes and conducted various usability tests. Once the designs were finalized, I created the mockups and then finally moved to prototyping.

User research highlights

84%

of users browsed video content online. 81% browsed new and social networks.

61%

of users download content that interests them directly to their computer rather than using an application or website.

71%

of respondents bookmark content using their broswer rather than an application or a website.

Users wanted to save and create content quickly and be able to collaborate with ease. Social registration with similar sites was typically a hassle and managing bookmarks was a common problem as well.

Competitive analysis

I set up a competitive analysis of Box, Evernote, and Dropbox comparing the key features and differentiating factors, how they position themselves in the market and who their primary audiences were. I found an opportunity to simplify the user experience while focusing on a smaller number of key features

User Personas

I started creating user personas to determine and finalize requirements, validate concepts and develop a script for usability tests. Three personas were developed from the user research. Julie wanted a centralized way of organizing inspiration that’s not time consuming and has easy-to-use tools. Robert wants to share inspiration with his team and organize their content into groups while having the ability to write and share notes. Sun wants to keep track of bookmarked content and be able to view it from work or from home.

persona details

User Stories and Flows

The user stories and flows were then created so the structure of the site would start to develop. I incorporated user feedback into flow edits after a detailed analysis of initial results. This allowed for a good foundation before continuing on with the development of the site.

Branding

Sites that save content, such as personal photos and notes, need to be trustworthy and reliable. A deep blue was chosen as the main color, which was accented by a strawberry red for any calls to action. The Gotham font family was the chosen typography, which gave the design a strong but modern feel and allowed for a good visual hierarchy throughout the site.

Logo

I started by sketching out ideas and eventually landed on a simple, yet effective icon. The logo is a minimalist icon with the “Capsule” text inside, reminiscent of putting items into a time capsule. I wanted the logo to work in various sizes and with various color schemes, which is why there was a focus put on simplicity.

Wireframes

I created user stories and began developing the sitemap and lo-fidelity mockups on Balsamiq. After initial usability testing and feedback, I moved to high-fidelity mockups and created a clickable prototype for additional usability testing.

Mockups

After receiving user feedback from the usability tests, I made design improvements to button placements on various pages and ensured the user’s interaction with the site was as clear and efficient as possible. I then finalized the designs in Photoshop and began the HTML, CSS and Javascript development.

Conclusion

The final prototype was sent to users to gather additional feedback. The results showed that the users had a clear understanding of Capsule’s purpose and were able to group and create content with ease, collaborate with peers in an effective way and complete tasks with minimal effort.

This first project taught me a great deal as I was new to the world of design and coding. It illustrated the importance of planning and understanding the user and how to tailor the site to provide the best experience possible. I was surprised by some of the user feedback, which was helpful while designing the user flows and ultimately, the experience the users had with Capsule.

I learned to take the business requirements and user feedback and meld them into one comprehensive experience. I’ll take the lessons from this project and will tweak and develop them so the process continues evolving.
